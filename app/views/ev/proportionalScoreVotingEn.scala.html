Mixed Member Proportional with Score Voting


Mixed Member Proportional with Score Voting mostly has the same rules as Mixed Member Proportionnal used in many countries (among them Germany and New Zealand).

See This Wikipedia article explains the standard mixed member proportional method like our current system (Plurality) it uses a uninominal (vote for only candidate) ballot. The basic steps in the standard mixed member system are :

  • 1) Two thirds of the parliament's seats are assigned to a territorial unit (an electoral district), the remaining seats are called compensatory seats, the are redistributed in order to have a proportionnal result.
  • 2) The elector has a double ballot, and casts two votes
    • i) For a representant in his electoral district
    • ii) For his favotite party at the national (or provincial) level.
  • 3) Territorial seats are assigned by counting the territorial ballots with the standard plurality (non proportionnal) method.
  • 4) Party ballots are counted, and the percentage obtained by each party are used to determin how the compensatory seats are distributed, the rule of distribution seeks to minimize the difference in percentage between the percentage of seats (territorial plus compensatory) that each party obtains is as close as possible to the percentages expressed in the party ballots. It is this step that ensures proportionality.

  • 3)Territorial seats are assigned using the territorial ballots (with standard score voting)
